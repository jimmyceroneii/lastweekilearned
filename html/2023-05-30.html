Idea of the Week: We are what we dream of

One of my best friends stunned me with a thought experiment yesterday. He laid out a scenario where we controlled our dreams. We could make them whatever we wanted. At first, we’d likely fulfill all of our greatest fantasies. We’d gorge on hedonism, only to find with the endless time our dreams provided, they felt empty. We’d then move to higher pursuits, chasing consistency and meaning.

I thought the experiment would end there. But my friend then made the same point Esther Perelle does in Mating in Captivity: we would grow bored. Even with all our “true” needs met, we would find ourselves drowning in predictability. What we’d turn to after all our dreams years of bliss is randomness.

We don’t really want all our dreams, both shallow and deep, to come true. We don’t want to know the outcome. We don’t actually want control, even as we fight tooth and nail for it.

That’s the thought experiment. I’m not sure what to make of it, to be honest. I don’t take enough time to dream, let alone imagine my dreams and those beyond them coming true. But this experiment contains the familiar weight of truth.

And do be honest, it exposes most of my machinations as folly. One of the central tenants of No More Mr. Nice Guy is that the smooth life is an illusion. There is no amount of “right” we can do to get it problem free. What blows my mind though, is that the problem free life is not truly desirable. We crave uncertainty, adventure, discovery. Knowing it all is no fun.

This little thought experiment has me thinking. What can I let go of? What bit of control I feel keeps me safe is in fact the very thing holding me back?
